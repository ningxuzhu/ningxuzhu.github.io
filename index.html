Rare Earth Ion Qubits
Rare earth ion impurity atoms in crystalline hosts feature electronic spin and optical transitions with long coherence times and the potential for strong interactions. Efficiently isolating and manipulating individual rare earth impurities is an outstanding challenge, however, since their optical transitions are typically E1-forbidden and therefore slow.
Our research integrates single rare earth ions into nanophotonic optical structures that enhance emission rates by orders of magnitude. We focus on developing telecom-wavelength photon sources and quantum memories based on single Erbium ions.
